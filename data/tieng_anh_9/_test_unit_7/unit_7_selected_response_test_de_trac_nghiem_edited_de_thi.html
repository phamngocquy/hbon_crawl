Read the text about the eating habits of Vietnamese people and do a matching heading task. 


The eating habits of Vietnamese people


A. Vietnamese food are different from the North to the South. Northern food is often less spicy than that in other parts of the country. Meanwhile, the central part is well-known for its spicy food. Food in the South is often seasoned with more sugar.


B. Vietnamese people like to boil, steam, stew and stir-fry food. When cooking, they often add ingredients like onion, garlic, ginger and season food with fish sauce.


C. A typical Vietnamese meal consists of rice, vegetables soup, main dishes and pickles. Main dishes are different among families but may include a dish of fish, eggs, or meat and stir-fried vegetables. People also love eating fresh and raw vegetables. Fresh fruits and chè (sweet soup) are often served as desserts.


D. Dishes are usually put on a round tray. In the center is a small bowl of dipping sauce, often fish sauce with garlic, chili and lemon juice. Surrounding it are dishes of meat, fish, vegetables, etc. 


E. Most Vietnamese families sit at dining tables or on floor mats. There’s a custom that children invite adults before eating. Everyone has their own bowl of rice and share the main dishes. They use chopsticks to pick up food.


New words:

Spicy (a): cay

Pickles (n): dưa chua, dưa góp

Tray (n): mâm
