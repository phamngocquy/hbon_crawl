Last year, I was in Ha Tinh, and I will never forget the experience that i had there . I still remember it was 9 p.m. when it began raining heavily. After that, a typhoon hit the whole area and the ground began to shake like crazy. While I was panicking, everyone there had already managed to get out of the building. The building collapsed and I was stuck inside. I was so scared, but people told me that the rescue workers were on their way. The storm kept going throughout the night, and I was rescued the next morning. Not many people were injured, but there was huge damage to the houses. Thông tin

Information text

Below is a guideline of what you should do before, while and after an earthquake. Read the guideline and complete the task that follows.

An earthquake often comes without warnings, so it is best to prepare for it whenever possible. Make sure you have an emergency supply kit (a radio, a flashlight, medical supplies and extra batteries). Next, you should prepare a disaster plan of who to contact, where to meet your family and where to evacuate during the time of an earthquake.

When an earthquake strikes, stay inside. Running out is dangerous, because you might be injured when trees and buildings collapse. Remember to drop to the ground, go under a table, and hold on till the shaking has weakened. If you are outdoors, rush into public shelters and stay there till rescue workers arrive.

After an earthquake, check yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid for anyone who needs it. Next, turn on the radio to keep posted about the situation outside the shelter. After an earthquake, debris are scattered everywhere, so you should wear boots or thick shoes to prevent cutting your feet. Last, stay away from damaged areas like buildings and houses, because they might fall on you.
