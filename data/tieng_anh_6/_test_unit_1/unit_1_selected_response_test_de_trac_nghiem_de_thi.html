D. READING

Read the text below. 

Living at a boarding school

My name is Ginny and I am a student at a boarding school in the UK. 

Living at the boarding school is an interesting experience. I often get up at 7 o’clock every day and have an hour to do exercise before breakfast. There are less than 20 students in my class, so it doesn’t take much time for me to remember my classmates’ names. 

Like other boarding schools in the UK, my school also offers many different kinds of extracurricular activities. Students can choose to take part in an art club or some sport teams such as: football, taekwondo or marital arts. There is also a swimming class for beginners, but it only opens on Tuesday and Thursday.

Students must do all their homework before going to the class. I am not good at maths and physics, so I always ask Lily for help. Lily is my roommate and she comes from Scotland. 

The school usually prepares a meal at 10 p.m. My favourite choice is a cup of tea and some cheese cakes. Lights often go off around 11 p.m. so it’s time for all of us to go to sleep. Although I seldom go home, I still feel comfortable when living with my friends at the boarding school.
